Why did you make ClassyFi?

ClassyFi was a project of necessity! Research into open source social media big data led me to large datasets of photos that needed manual classification. There wasnâ€™t a good option on the market that allowed for dynamic group photo classification. So, I studied up and got to work! Thus, ClassyFi was born.

How did you make ClassyFi?

Front end: HTML, CSS, BULMA, Jinja, JavaScript (some JQuery)

Back end: Flask, SQLite (SQLAlchemy), Gspread (Google Sheet API V4), deployed on a uWSGI server behind an Nginx reverse proxy.

What are your goals for ClassyFi?

Ultimately, I want to turn ClassyFi into an online photo classification tool that anyone can use. This means two main things:

  1. Integrate dynamic front-end classification methodologies editable in real-time by users like you.
  2. Leverage modern ML technologies to optimize and simplify manual classifications.
As an active student, these goals might take some time! However, I remain dedicated and driven to make ClassyFi a prominent classification tool.
